Recipe #1

Fluffy Pancakes with Kefir

    Ingredients:

  • Time: 30 minute
  • Serves: for 2 people
Ingredients Quantity
Kefir (2.5–3.2%) – 250 g
Wheat flour – 230 g
Chicken egg – 1 pc
Sugar – 1–2 tbsp
Salt – a pinch
Baking soda – 0.5–1 tsp (without a heap)
Vegetable oil – for frying

Step 1: Do something.

Measure out the kefir and, stirring occasionally, heat it almost to a boil.

One of the “secrets” to making fluffy pancakes is using kefir with a fat content of 2.5% or higher. This doesn’t mean that you can’t make pancakes with low-fat kefir—you can—but with “full-fat” kefir they will always turn out a little fluffier and tastier.

Step 2:

Add the egg, a little sugar, and a pinch of salt to the hot kefir. Mix everything thoroughly until you get a smooth, even mixture. There’s no need to beat the ingredients, because the fluffiness of the pancakes in this recipe does not depend on the eggs

Another piece of folk wisdom: don’t add too much sugar to the batter. Too much sugar causes the pancakes to burn quickly on the outside before they have time to cook properly on the inside.

Step 3:

Sift the wheat flour twice. Make a small well in the center of the flour and pour the egg-and-kefir mixture into it. Mix everything thoroughly, trying to stir in one direction—either clockwise or counterclockwise.

It may seem like this detail belongs to the realm of superstition, but it doesn’t. When you mix the ingredients randomly, you unintentionally slightly whip the batter. Small air bubbles form, making the texture more uneven. By stirring in one direction, you combine the ingredients more gently, and the batter turns out smoother and glossier.

Step 4:

You should end up with a very thick batter. It shouldn’t pour, but rather cling slightly to the spoon and then slowly drip down. If needed, add a little more flour until you reach the desired consistency.

Step 5:

Gradually add the baking soda to the finished batter while stirring. Mix everything well again and let the batter rest for 10–15 minutes.

Step 6:

After that, do not stir the batter anymore. Gently scoop small portions with a spoon and place them into the heated vegetable oil. Fry the pancakes over medium or low heat.

To help the pancakes cook through faster and rise better, you can cover the pan with a lid for the first few minutes of frying.

Step 7:

When the batter on the surface of the pancakes becomes matte and slightly firm, flip them over and fry, uncovered, for a few more minutes until golden brown.

Step 8:

Gradually add the baking soda to the finished batter while stirring. Mix everything well again and let the batter rest for 10–15 minutes.

Step 9:

Enjoy your meal!
